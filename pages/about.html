About SafeSip

The science, the mission, and the technology behind the device

OUR MISSION

No one should fear what's in their drink.

SafeSip was founded after our co-founder's sister was hospitalized following a drink-spiking incident at a university event. The existing "solutions" — test strips, nail polishes, apps — were unreliable, slow, and socially awkward. We set out to build something that actually works.

Two years and 120,000 spectral training samples later, SafeSip is the first keychain-sized electrochemical biosensor with on-device AI classification, capable of detecting 47 controlled substances in under one second with 98.4% accuracy.

TECHNOLOGY

How It Actually Works

01

Three-Electrode Electrochemical Cell

SafeSip uses a working electrode (carbon), a reference electrode (Ag/AgCl), and a counter electrode (gold). When a droplet is applied, the device performs cyclic voltammetry — sweeping voltage from –0.6V to +1.2V and back in 400ms, recording the resulting current. This current vs. voltage profile is a unique fingerprint for every compound class.

02

On-Device Neural Network (ARM Cortex-M4)

The raw voltammogram (200 data points) is fed into a 4-layer, quantized INT8 neural network running on an ARM Cortex-M4F at 80MHz. Inference takes 180ms. The model was trained on 120,000 samples across 47 substance classes, including multiple concentration levels and common drink matrices (alcohol, carbonation, acidity).

03

Bluetooth 5.3 LE + Companion App

Results are transmitted via BLE to the companion app in <50ms. The app handles SMS dispatch, GPS logging, and the companion network protocol. All chemical data stays on the device — only the classification result and timestamp are transmitted.

04

Disposable Sensor Tips (60-Use)

Each sensor tip lasts 60 scans. Replacement tips ship monthly via subscription. The electrode surface is functionalized with molecularly imprinted polymers (MIPs) that enhance selectivity for high-priority targets like GHB and fentanyl analogs.

05

OTA Firmware & Library Updates

New compounds are added via monthly over-the-air updates. Our forensic chemistry team continuously monitors DEA drug seizure databases and academic literature to keep the detection library current. Users receive push notifications when new substances are added.
